# Hiring Assessment - AI Generated song detection

## AI-Original Pairwise Similarity

**Objective:** This assessment evaluates the candidate's ability to design and implement a robust **pairwise similarity metric** to detect and quantify the relationship between an original human-made track and a potential AI-generated counterpart. Instead of simple binary classification, the focus is on **relational modeling**: determining if Track B is a synthetic derivative, variation, or "hallucinated" generation based on Track A.

**Goal:** Develop a system that takes two full-length audio files as input and outputs a **Similarity (Attribution) Score**. This score must reflect the likelihood that the synthetic track is an attribution of the original, moving beyond short-clip matching to account for variations in tempo, arrangement, and spectral artifacts characteristic of modern generators like Suno and Udio.

---

### Dataset Overview:

Candidates should leverage a multi-source approach to ensure high-variance training and testing:

- **SONICS Dataset:** The primary source for large-scale, end-to-end synthetic songs (97k+ tracks) generated by platforms like Suno and Udio ([awsaf49/sonics · Datasets at Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/datasets/awsaf49/sonics)).

- **FakeMusicCaps Dataset:** A specialized dataset for analyzing text-to-music (TTM) outputs, containing 10-second clips regenerated via various state-of-the-art models for fine-grained artifact analysis ([FakeMusicCaps](https://github.com/Placeholder-FakeMusicCaps)).

- **MIPPIA Dataset (SMP):** Essential for the attribution task and evaluating melodic similarity.

  **Note:** The MIPPIA dataset (Similar Music Pair) provides a dedicated **download function** (`download.py`) to fetch the original high-quality audio files from their source URLs, that can be used for the other datasets as well ([GitHub - Mippia/smp_dataset](https://github.com/Mippia/smp_dataset)), libraries needed: 'pandas', 'yt-dlp' and installation of 'ffmpeg'.

### Assessment Instructions:

- **Data Exploration:** Analyze the dataset and provide insights.

- **Feature Engineering:** Propose and justify the use of specific audio or not features. Consider whether standard features (MFCCs, Mel-spectrograms) are sufficient or if higher-order features (Phase-Continuity, Harmonic-to-Noise Ratio) are required.

- **Model Architecture:** Design a solution capable of handling variable-length audio (avg. 3 minutes). Candidates should address how they prevent "window-bias" (only looking at the first 30 seconds).

- **Bonus: Attribution Logic:** Implement a similarity metric to evaluate the relationship between pairs of tracks.
